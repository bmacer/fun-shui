# Fun Shui Ideation: Strategic Next Steps

**Run ID:** fs_ideation
**Date:** May 19, 2026

## The Strategy
Fun Shui's goal is to transition from a landing page to a functional social business. The most critical lever is a **low-friction group booking system** combined with an **automated digital reveal** (3D models) that fuels social sharing. By emphasizing the "luxury" of staff movers and the "discovery" of sipping and walking, we differentiate from existing Asheville activities.

---

## Top 7 Survivor Ideas

### 1. The "Group Split" Payment Portal
*   **The Idea:** A booking funnel that allows one organizer (the "Head Bridesmaid") to reserve a room with a deposit, then generates a unique link for individual participants to pay their own ticket price.
*   **Warrant:** `External:` Apps like "Batch" and "Venmo" are the standard for groups. Solving the "Venmo Chase" is the #1 pain point for bachelorette organizers.
*   **Why it Matters:** Frictionless booking leads to higher conversion rates for larger groups who would otherwise stall on payment logistics.

### 2. The "Design Sommelier" 3D Handoff
*   **The Idea:** Automate the digital reveal by having the "Staff Movers" use an iPad/Phone to scan the room (using LiDAR/Photogrammetry) during the final "Sip and Walk." The 3D model is processed and emailed instantly as the participants leave.
*   **Warrant:** `Reasoned:` High-tech artifacts are most valuable when the "emotional high" of the event is still active. 
*   **Why it Matters:** Instant delivery turns customers into immediate social promoters before they even leave the building.

### 3. "Luxury Butler" Reframing of Movers
*   **The Idea:** Rebrand the "Staff Movers" as "Fun Shui Design Butlers." They wear branded gold/purple attire and their movement of furniture is choreographed as part of the "show," ensuring participants feel pampered, not just assisted.
*   **Warrant:** `Reasoned:` For a bachelorette party, "strapping men moving furniture" is part of the entertainment value, not just a logistical necessity.
*   **Why it Matters:** It transforms a labor cost into a high-value brand experience.

### 4. The "Vintage Passport" Flight Mat
*   **The Idea:** A physical or digital "Tasting Mat" (analogy to wine flights) where participants "collect" the eras of the furniture they select (e.g., Mid-Century Modern, Victorian, Art Deco). 
*   **Warrant:** `Analogy:` Wine flights succeed because they give people a sense of discovery and accomplishment.
*   **Why it Matters:** Encourages exploration of the *whole* inventory, increasing the time spent and social engagement.

### 5. Liquid Palette: The "Chroma Pour"
*   **The Idea:** The wine tasting is tied to the design process. Choosing a "mood" for your room (e.g., "Deep Moody Purple") recommends a specific bold red wine flight to sip while designing.
*   **Warrant:** `Assumption-breaking:` Wine shouldn't just be *present*; it should be an *input* to the design process.
*   **Why it Matters:** Deepens the "Sip and Design" tagline into a cohesive, immersive experience.

### 6. Interactive "Inventory Walk" Previewer
*   **The Idea:** On the website, before booking, users can "walk" a 3D gallery of the actual vintage inventory available. 
*   **Warrant:** `Direct:` Requirement for "Vast Inventory" documentation. 
*   **Why it Matters:** Proves the value of the experience and serves as a powerful marketing "hook" for design enthusiasts.

### 7. The "Social Highlight" AI Reel (V2)
*   **The Idea:** Use the 3D room scan data + floor camera footage to auto-generate a 15-second "Design Transformation" video (Before vs After) for every group.
*   **Warrant:** `Direct:` Stretch goal from requirements.
*   **Why it Matters:** TikTok/Instagram reels are the primary discovery engine for bachelorette activities in 2026.

---

## Rejected Ideas
*   *Hologram Inventory:* Rejected as it removes the "tactile play" and "vintage Asheville" core value.
*   *Professional Design Services:* Rejected to avoid "Expert vs Play" friction; the focus is fun, not perfection.
*   *User Account Dashboards:* Rejected in favor of direct email links to keep the tech stack lean for launch.
